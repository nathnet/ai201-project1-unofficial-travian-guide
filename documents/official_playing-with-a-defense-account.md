# Playing With a Defense Account

> Source: Travian: Legends Support  
> URL: https://support.travian.com/en/articles/184-playing-with-a-defense-account

---

Defense accounts play a crucial role in Travian: Legends. A strong defender can influence the outcome of battles, protect alliance assets, and keep key areas secure. This guide explains how to develop a defense-focused account, how to specialize your villages, and how to adapt to different gameworld types.

## What Is Expected From a Defensive Player?

As a defender, your value comes from helping your alliance react quickly and reliably. You are generally expected to:

- Send defensive support when requested.
- Snipe waves when needed.
- Stay available during critical hours or make sure sitter access covers those times.

These expectations ensure your alliance can respond to enemy operations effectively.

---

## Defense Account Specializations

There are several common ways to specialize a defense account. The best choice depends on your alliance, nearby threats, available resources, and your chosen tribe.

### **1. Infantry-Only Defense**

Useful for:

- Early game, when infantry is cheap and quick to train
- World Wonder and artefact defense
- Situations where defense travel distances are short
- Battles where sheer defensive numbers matter more than speed

Development notes:

- Train infantry in almost every village
- Build and upgrade Barracks and Hospital
- Skip Stables
- Tournament Square around level 15

### **2. Cavalry-Only Defense**

Useful for:

- Large or spread-out alliances
- Gameworlds with high activity
- Experienced players who can react quickly

Development notes:

- Train cavalry in every second or third village
- Build and upgrade Stables and Hospital
- Skip Barracks
- Tournament Square around level 15

### **3. Mixed Infantry and Cavalry**

A balanced option that fits most gameworld situations.
Less ideal early game due to resource cost.

Development notes:

- Train both infantry and cavalry in the same village
- Upgrade both Barracks and Stables
- Can combine with other approaches (e.g., only a few villages produce both types)

### **4. Hybrid Defense Account**

A defense-focused account that also trains a small attacking army in one village.

Useful for:

- Players with strong economies
- Alliance operations where occasional offensive help matters
- Experienced players who want to contribute in all areas

Development notes:

- One offensive village with infantry, cavalry, rams, catapults, and chiefs
- Defensive villages follow one of the earlier specializations
- Requires more gold than a pure defense account

*Note:* Most defensive accounts mix tactics rather than sticking to just one. Combining infantry-only, cavalry-only, and mixed villages is common and often effective. Always adapt to the gameworld situation.

## Developing a Defense Account

Key principles for economic and military balance:

- Keep approximately **40% of your production** for continued development.
- Avoid training large troop numbers until at least one (ideally two) villages are fully built.
- If you spawn in a hostile area, these rules may need to be ignored for safety.
- Starting with an infantry-only focus helps you build an early defensive pool without slowing your economy too much.
- Capitals rarely have enough building slots for proper defense specialization—use them for resource storage and development instead, and focus defense production in nearby villages.

### Hero Equipment

Defensive players benefit from a diverse set of hero items to boost troop performance. You can find recommendations in the hero equipment guide.

## Defense on Special Gameworlds

Different features in special servers require adjustments to your defensive approach.

### **Troop Merging & Forwarding**

Forwarding allows you to reposition defense between villages without recalling it home.
Tactics often include:

- Settling further from central battles
- Keeping 1–2 villages in “hot zones” for fast reaction

### **Ancient Powers**

If your alliance controls small ancient powers, every defense village must have a **treasury level 10** to activate them.

### **European Map & Region Control**

Defenders must help secure regions.
On these servers:

- Accounts are more spread across the map
- Settling in **clusters** (groups of villages close together) is strongly recommended
- Once you have a four-village cluster, you can begin training defense reliably

---

## Summary

Defense accounts are essential to alliance success. Whether you focus on infantry, cavalry, or a mix, your main goal is to contribute reliable, well-timed support. Effective defense depends on planning, coordination, and adaptation to the ongoing server situation. By developing your villages carefully and choosing a specialization that fits your alliance’s needs, you help shape the outcome of the game for your entire team.
